Core Game Rules
The Goal: Eliminate low-value boxes and keep high-value boxes in play, aiming for a high cash offer or a high value in the final box.
The Play: The contestant chooses boxes to open, revealing the cash amounts inside, which are removed from the board.
The Banker's Offer: At the end of each round (after a specific number of boxes are opened), the Banker makes a cash offer based on the remaining amounts, asking, "Deal or No Deal?".
Deal: The player accepts the money and the game ends.
No Deal: The player rejects the offer and continues playing.
The End Game: The game continues until only two boxes remain (the player's box and one other). The player can then swap their box or stay with the original.
Final Opening: The player opens their chosen box to reveal their final prize. 

Round Structure
The game typically involves five initial rounds (opening 5, 3, 3, 3, 3 boxes respectively), followed by single-box rounds when few boxes remain. 

Other Rules and Features
The Offer Button (introduced later): A button that could be pressed once, forcing the Banker to make an offer, provided the contestant correctly guessed the offer within a 10% margin.
The Banker: An unseen antagonist who communicates via telephone, trying to buy the contestant's box for the lowest possible amount.
